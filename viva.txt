# DAY 1

### What is your project?

Faculty Recruitment Portal.

A system that helps process faculty resumes/CVs and extract relevant information automatically for easier candidate evaluation.

---

### Why is this project needed?

Faculty resumes are long and contain publications, research work, teaching experience, certifications, etc.

Manual review takes significant time.

Automation can speed up screening and information extraction.

| ------------------- | ------------------- |
| 1-2 pages           | 5-20 pages          |
| Skills focused      | Research focused    |
| Projects            | Publications        |
| Industry experience | Teaching + Research |

# DAY 3

---

### What is OCR?

Optical Character Recognition.

Converts text present inside images into machine-readable text.

---

### What is Tesseract OCR?

Open-source OCR engine developed by Google.

Used to extract text from scanned documents.

---

### Why do we need OCR?

Scanned PDFs contain images, not actual text.

Normal text extraction fails.

OCR converts image text into real text.

---

### What is spaCy?

Python NLP library.

Used for:

* Tokenization
* NER
* POS tagging
* Parsing

---

### What is NER?

Named Entity Recognition.

Identifies entities like:

* Person names
* Organizations
* Locations
* Dates

Example:

"John works at Google."

↓

John → PERSON

Google → ORG

---

### What is PyMuPDF?

Python library for PDF processing.

Can:

* Open PDFs
* Extract text
* Extract images
* Read metadata

---

### Why did you study PyMuPDF?

Because digital PDFs can be processed directly without OCR.

### What experiment did you perform?

PyMuPDF text extraction experiment.

---

### What PDFs did you test?

1. Digital faculty resume

7 pages

2. Scanned resume

1 page

---

### Result?

Digital:

10842 characters extracted.

Scanned:

Only 2 characters extracted.

---

### Conclusion?

PyMuPDF works well for digital PDFs.

Fails for scanned PDFs.

---

### Why?

Digital PDF contains actual text.

Scanned PDF contains images.

---

### Why is OCR needed?

Because scanned PDFs contain no machine-readable text.

### Why didn't OCR run?

### Difference?

↓

### What spaCy experiment did you run?

NER on extracted resume text.

---

### What output did you get?

Example:

```text
Department of Information Technology → ORG
```

```text
Andhra Pradesh → PERSON
```

Wrong classification.

---

### What did you learn?

Pre-trained models make mistakes.

Need custom rules or filtering.

---

### Why was Andhra Pradesh classified incorrectly?

Generic models are trained on broad datasets and sometimes misclassify resume-specific content.

---

### Why not rely entirely on spaCy?

Because resumes have unique formatting.

Need regex + NLP together.

# VERY COMMON FINAL QUESTIONS

### Why Python?

Rich ecosystem:

* Flask
* spaCy
* PyMuPDF
* pytesseract

---

### Why not Java?

Python has stronger NLP and OCR ecosystem and faster prototyping.

---

### What is JSON?

JavaScript Object Notation.

Structured data format.

Example:

```json
{
  "name":"Tia",
  "email":"tia@gmail.com"
}
```

---

### What is the biggest challenge in resume parsing?

Variability.

Every resume looks different.

---

### Why combine regex and NLP?

Regex:

Good for fixed patterns.

NLP:

Good for contextual information.

Together they perform better.

---

### What is your biggest Week 1 finding?

Digital PDFs can be parsed directly, but scanned PDFs require OCR.
